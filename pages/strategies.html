Strategies

  • Due to the game relying upon randomness, playing an optimum strategy does not guarantee a win.
  • Due to how probabilities work, rolling more dice increases the chances of losing one's turn.
  • It is possible (not probable) to reach the goal score without ever taking a hold action.
  • It is also possible to roll all 1s, and never score any points in a game.
Instructions Rationale
Score Goals Pick a score goal for each round. If you make your goal stop. Using a 6-sided die, 20 seems to work best. Picking a goal that balances the risk against the reward is the key to this strategy.
Scoring Turns Choose a number of scoring turns to have, and make the needed number of points to reach 100 your goal for each round. Due to the randomness of the die, over-scoring your goal is quite common. Being able to adjust your goal per round allows the player to stop earlier, reducing the risk.
Turn Buffer Select a number of turns and a number to serve as a buffer. Follow the "Scoring Turns" strategy, but allow a range around each goal the size you selected. If you take a score within the leeway below the buffer, increase the next goal. If you take points greater than the goal, decrease the next goal, staying within the buffer. Allowing the goal to be flexible, adapting to the scores you have already gained, allows you to further reduce the risks.
Competitive Goals Keep track of the other players score, increasing your goal whenever they pass you, decreasing your goal whenever you pass them. Goals must be at least the selected score base (19 should be pretty good). Keep rolling if either player is within a certain distance from the end (31 suggested). Keeping track of the other player's score allows you to adapt to the situation. If the other player is roughly one turn away from winning, you have nothing to lose from continuous rolling.
