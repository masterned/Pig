Strategies

  • Due to the game relying upon randomness, playing an "optimum" strategy does not guarantee a win.
  • Due to how probabilities work, rolling more dice increases the chances of losing one's turn.
  • It is possible (not probable) to reach the goal score without ever taking a hold action.
  • It is also possible to roll all 1s, and never score any points in a game.
  • The strategies outlined here are optimized for the original game. Additional rules may make them less efficient.
  • The game has a slight bias towards the first player to roll.

Score Goals

Pick a score goal for each round. If you make your goal stop. Around 20 seems to work best. Picking a goal that balances the risk against the reward is the key to this strategy.

Scoring Turns

Choose a number of scoring turns to have, and divide 100 by that number to get your goal for each round. No matter the goal, it is more common to bust on a round or overshoot it than land on it exactly. Adjust the goal by dividing the number of points left by the number of round remaining. This strategy allows the player to reduce their risk each turn by leveraging the score they have already accumulated.

Turn Buffer

Use the "Scoring Turns" strategy but factor in a score buffer. Give yourself a specific amount of leeway round the goal, and adjust accordingly. You will need to account for the buffer when calculating your next goal. This allows the goals more flexibility, reducing the player's risk further.

Competitive Goals

Keep track of the other players score, increasing your goal whenever they pass you, decreasing your goal whenever you pass them. Goals must be at least the selected score base (19 should be pretty good). Keep rolling if either player is within a certain distance from the end (31 suggested). Keeping track of the other player's score allows you to adapt to the situation. If the other player is roughly one turn away from winning, you have nothing to lose from continuous rolling.
