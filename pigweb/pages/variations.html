Variations

2-Dice Pig

This version is nearly identical to the original. As it's name suggests, instead of rolling a single die at a time, each player rolls a pair of dice. The points are calculated as normal with the added rule of double ones removing a player's total score.

Big Pig

This one is similar to the 2-Dice Rules. Players roll two dice, but doubles double the roll score, and double 1s do not ruin the player's total score but add 25 to their temporary score. There is a caveat, rolling a double forces the player to roll again.

Hog

According to other sources on the web, Hog can be played with any of the other variants' rules. The original scoring system is the only one that does not cause ambiguity in this version. In Hog, players choose the number of dice they are going to roll at the beginning of their turns. The dice can all be rolled at the same time.

Skunk

Skunk has two variations itself, but both versions have the same scoring rules as 2-Dice Pig. One version of Skunk is played with only five rounds. A scorecard with columns for each of the letters in SKUNK is typically used to keep track of the scores in each round. Boxed versions of the game also include dice with skunk symbols replacing the 1s.

The other version does not have a turn limit, but it ends after a bonus round that occurs after one of the players gets 100 or more points. A player wins in this variation if they have the highest score after the bonus round. In the bonus round, all players (except for the one that caused the bonus round) are given one more chance to get the highest score.

Pass the Pigs

Pass the Pigs is a boxed gamed with almost identical rules to 2-Dice Pig. It uses novelty, pig-shaped dice with unique scoring determined by the position of the pigs after they are rolled. The game includes an optional rule "Hog Call" which allows players to risk some of their points for a chance to steal points from the player rolling. A more detailed rules document can be found here.

Positions Score
One pig on their left side, the other on their right. 0 points. End turn.
Both pigs on same side. 1 point.
One pig standing or on its back, other on side. 5 points.
Both pigs standing or on their backs. 20 points.
One pig on its snout, the other on side. 10 points.
Both pigs on snouts. 40 points.
One propped between its snout, ear, and leg. Other on its side. 15 points.
Both in snout-ear-leg position. 60 points.
Pigs are touching after roll. Lose all points. End turn.
One pig standing on the other. Player is eliminated from the game.
